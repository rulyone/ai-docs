# The Meaning of Life

A one-page meditation rendered as Bauhaus design. The page treats the question itself as a composition problem: form follows function, the function being thought.

## Premise

There is no single answer waiting to be uncovered. The meaning of life is not a hidden object; it is a constructed one. Like a Bauhaus chair, it is assembled from primary materials according to honest principles, and its value comes from the assembly.

## The Three Primaries

Bauhaus reduced color to red, yellow, blue. A life can be reduced, with similar honesty, to three primaries.

| Primary | Element | Function |
| ------- | ------- | -------- |
| Red | Attention | What you look at, you become. |
| Yellow | Connection | What you love, you are responsible for. |
| Blue | Craft | What you make, makes you. |

Mixed in different proportions, these produce every meaningful life that has ever been lived. No one has discovered a fourth.

## The Three Forms

- **Circle** — the recurring day. Wake, work, eat, sleep. Most of life is this. Honor it; do not flee it.
- **Square** — the structures you build. Habits, relationships, projects, promises kept. Stable. Stackable. Load-bearing.
- **Triangle** — the direction you point. A vector, not a destination. The arrow matters more than the target.

## The Manifesto

1. Meaning is not found. It is fabricated, daily, from materials at hand.
2. The question "what is the meaning of life" is the wrong shape. The right shape is "what am I making of this one."
3. Beauty is not decoration. It is the visible evidence of care.
4. Reduce until what remains is essential. Then reduce once more.
5. Form follows function. Function is: to live, to love, to leave the room better arranged than you found it.

## Closing

You will not solve life. You will compose it. The primaries are given. The proportions are yours.

---

*Designed in the spirit of the Bauhaus, 1919–1933. Form follows function; function is care.*
